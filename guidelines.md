# Hospital Triage Protocols

## Priority Levels

### Critical (Level 1)
- **Definition**: Immediate threat to life.
- **Criteria**: Cardiac arrest, severe trauma, respiratory failure, stroke symptoms (FAST), chest pain suggesting MI.
- **Action**: Immediate ICU/Resus admission.

### High (Level 2)
- **Definition**: Urgent but stable for now. Potential to deteriorate.
- **Criteria**: High fever > 40C, severe pain, dehydration, confused state.
- **Action**: Urgent assessment within 30 mins.

### Medium (Level 3)
- **Definition**: Stable, requires multiple resources.
- **Criteria**: Fracture, abdominal pain, simple wounds requiring suturing.
- **Action**: Assessment within 60 mins.

### Low (Level 4)
- **Definition**: Minor ailment.
- **Criteria**: Cold/Flu, medication refill.
- **Action**: Wait times may apply.

## Resource Allocation
- ICU beds are reserved for Critical patients ONLY.
- If ICU is full, Critical patients must be stabilized in ED and transferred.
